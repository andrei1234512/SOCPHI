Legal Protections in the Philippines
As a Filipino, these laws protect you from identity theft and privacy violations.
RA 10173

Data Privacy Act of 2012

Protects personal information collected by government and private entities. Mandates consent before collecting data. Enforced by the National Privacy Commission (NPC).

RA 10175

Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012

Criminalizes identity theft, computer fraud, illegal access, and online libel. Identity theft carries up to 6 years imprisonment. Report to PNP-ACG or NBI Cybercrime Division.

RA 8484

Access Devices Regulation Act

Penalizes unauthorized use of credit cards, ATM cards, and other access devices. Covers credit card fraud, skimming, and identity theft involving financial instruments.

BSP Circular 1140

Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas Rules

BSP Circular 1140 strengthens consumer protection for digital financial services. Banks must implement multi-factor authentication and fraud monitoring systems.
